AskAway - Easy access to experts at your convenience

INTRODUCTION

The project was done as part of the UX design training at Career Foundry which followed a fixed course plan. It was executed in a span of 6 months.

It includes designing a web app. The project also includes coming up with the concept, app name and logo, problem statement, competitive research, user flows, creating low to high definition prototypes and user testing iterations.

Working on this project helped me deepen my knowledge in the various techniques used during UX designing and also impress the power of UX to influence and help users to have a wonderful experience while doing their activities.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

My user needs to be able to contact an expert whenever he/she needs to make decisions and that his/her data is in safe hands. The user might not be tech-savvy or comfortable with technology.

We will know this to be true when 75% of the app user posts positive reviews start recommending the app to other users.


"Listing down the challenges anticipated and the corresponding solutions to each of them help breakdown the objective to create a simple but effective problem statement."


OPPORTUNITIES IN CHALLENGES

  1. Access to an expert - any time - any place

    Engaging with an expert online removes the constraints of travel and place. Often the need to go see an expert makes people rely on information available online which often needs to be validated.

  2. Chat v/s Voice

    Some people are comfortable talking about problems, while some are not. The option of chatting with the expert gives them the confidence to share problems with a stranger. As a rapport is built, they can switch to voice call.

  3. Educating by being transparent

    Clients may have reservations on the amount of data to be shared on a chat service. On the other hand, there would be clients are not very internet savvy on what can be or cannot be shared on the chat. By being transparent about the way data is used and other aspects like pricing, we gain the trust of the client.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

I created competitive profiles - on product objective, target audience, marketing & overall strategy, SWOT profile, usability, layout, navigation, compatibility, content, design, performance, ratings. These helped me identify the pain points of the existing products and how to use it to benefit my target audience. User reviews give a far greater insight to the pros and cons of the product


"I take the competition seriously!! Some competitors do not have or share data you want to analyse, so in future I would identify at least 3 – 4 competitors if possible."

USER INTERVIEWS AND SURVEYS

For this project I needed a pool of people from all walks of life. I used social media to reach out to see the varied responses from people of different ages and countries and find their needs w.r.t. the app. Armed with feedback from around 53 participants I streamlined questions for the 3 planned interviews which increased my understanding of the user requirements.


"Focus group research was used to help with info requiring further clarity. Affinity maps were used to analyse the inputs to highlight the behaviour and immediate requirements of the users."

Insights from the research

  • Google is a favourite go-to tool to find solutions and advice on various areas in their lives. People are not convinced of authenticity and reliability and check multiple sites to conclude with the right answer.

  • Accessibility to lot of experts in the comfort of your home is attractive and less time consuming is the USP of the app. The app needs to be a lean, glitch free & safe app. People are willing to pay a moderately fair amount of money for people to answer their questions.

  • Messaging and the possibility of sending attachments is a major need. Adding voice and video ensures more choice for the user. Samples of answered questions by experts is a great way of generating a feeling of trust in the expert.​​​​​​​

USER PERSONAS

I created user personas to give a human touch to the user and understanding their goals, needs, motivations and frustrations. Understanding these will help create a more meaningful design for the user.

USER FLOWS

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

I used a Digital Card Sorting Test - OptimalSort to streamline the sitemap. Since I had to do the test with less than 10 participants, this tool worked well in its free mode.

I made a list of all the content and categories w.r.t. the user stories and flows and sitemap and came up with 27 cards. I chose the hybrid card sort as a starting point for the test. There were some obvious categories that I had envisioned and wanted the users to use these and categorize the rest of the cards according to their comfort of navigation. The messages to the participants had to be short and effective as the instructions were sent via email or electronic messages and I have no feedback on clarity.


"The card sort results were not very different from my own. This could be as participants preferred the predefined groups and did not create new groups. However there are some outliers that made me rethink some terms and structure and the sitemap was refined."

LOW DEFINITION WIREFRAMES

I sketched low definition wireframes using a paper template. Also transferred it to digital using the software Balsamiq.


"Rapid 8 is a very useful technique to use when you want to finalise on a design of an element or layout. I have used this technique through out the lifecycle of this project. It is very important to keep a balance between the information and space on both version to avoid too much info on a single screen. Sketching screens to create entire user flows is a very time intensive process. Elements that are repetitive are representational and not detailed at this stage.""

HIGH DEFINITION WIREFRAMES

I used Adobe Xd for creating high definition wireframes and the interactive prototypes. The wireframes in the prototype shared have been subjected to 2 iterations of reviews, usability testing and refining.


REFINING THE DESIGNS - DESIGN STYLE GUIDE

In addition to the tests and reviews, the design style guide is used to ensure that element styles are consistent across the product.

The colours and the visual elements used here are simple and elegant. The central theme colour for AskAway is a golden-brown hue. This colour evokes feelings of compassion, passion and wisdom, the core essence to be projected.

REFINING THE DESIGNS - TESTING AND FEEDBACK

Breaking down the test into multiple activities helped me to make decisions required for the test quite easily. For e.g. Decisions on recording equipment and remote monitoring with the Covid-19 situation.

  • An Elaborate Test Script

    I included check boxes and other instructions for the moderator to fill in after each step, so that I do not forget any question or feedback during the test. Conducting tests using a test script helped keep the whole exercise formal and people responded in a much more professional way than having an informal session especially since the test participants also included friends/family.

  • Feedback

    Errors and negative observations were rated from critical to minor using affinity maps. Critical and major errors were fixed, while some observations were noted but no action was taken at this stage. A negative observation raised was found to be due to lack of clear instructions for the task and the test script was updated accordingly for future test cycles.


An important observation made by one tester was the the calendar element might be too tiny for some users. I redesigned the element altogether as I think its a very valid observation that can prove critical to the app usage. The time elements are now bigger and the calendar can be scrolled down to reveal the remaining time slots.


WHAT I LEARNED FROM THIS PROJECT​​​​​​​​​​​​​​

  • Baby Steps

    To remember to breakdown activities to smaller manageable tasks. This helps me focus and plan better.

  • Fast Decisions

    Using techniques like crazy 8 to visualize various possibilities. Employing these techniques not just for prototyping but also in other design areas helps to reduce time taken in contemplating over decisions.

  • Documentation

    I realized that a rough draft of the design language documentation should be made available as early as possible in the project life cycle of prototyping, testing and refining. This would help set standards as soon as the high level wireframe are made and reduce a lot of rework. In this project I had a lot of different font families and sizes across the screen which had to be revisited.

  • Participant Awareness

    The test participants of this project were not very aware of the aim of usability testing. This caused many participants to be distracted by the non-Functioning components of the prototype. I would create and share a short video clip explaining the expectations from this test using a different example. This would be useful especially in case of remote testing.

  • Reviews

    Peer reviews are very important as they bring out totally different perspective to your designs.

  • Teamwork

    Collaboration is the keyword to a successful project. It is important to document and collate all information required ready to be handed over to the engineering team. These documents need to be updated on a regular basis
