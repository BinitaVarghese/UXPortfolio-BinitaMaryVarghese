Case Study - Redesign of User Purchase Flow

INTRODUCTION

The project was a redesign requirement by a firm looking for an alternative approach to their product buying user flow.

My goal as a UX Designer was to develop high-definition prototypes to showcase my approach. This case study used my analytical skills to understand the shortcomings of the existing design and come up with a clean and sharp design that helps the user make informed decisions. I spent around 3 to 4 days to complete this case study.

Skills used -

  • Competitive Analysis
  • User flows and Concept sketches
  • Persona hypothesis
  • Wireframing and Prototyping
  • Design Documentation & Presentation

PROBLEM STATEMENT

“To assist the customer who is looking for a smart meter to track their PV system - by helping them choose the right meter for their requirements and guiding them through the entire purchase sequence."

PRODUCT RESEARCH

The existing approach listed all the products on a single page. There was too much information about the product, its features and the services provided thrown at the user.

My immediate reaction was to create a page to present the available products as thumbnails and then click on the thumbnails to elaborate on the details of the product. I also intended to display a comparison chart so that the customers can see the haves and have-nots between the various products.

However on comparing the products and its features, I realised that the products are not comparable and have specific characteristics to it. In short,the products were not interchangeable.

“Listing down the different products and its features helped me focus and narrow down the problem at hand”

USER FLOW

Based on the list of all the information relevant to each product and its features a user flow was created. The idea here was to present the best option for the user requirement instead of presenting all the options.

Finding common ground and relevant crisp input from users can put users at ease, especially when you have lots of options for a particular item user want to purchase.

WIREFRAMING

High fidelity wireframes were built based on the user flow and an interactive prototype was created to visualise the flow better.

The objective was to NOT overwhelm the user with too much information but at the same time keeping all required information close enough to find at a click.

Keypoints:

(a) Prominently displayed features and service offers throughout the buying flow, so as to reassure the customer at all times. In the mobile version this data is displayed as a pop-up before the information gathering process.

(b) Segregated information collection with multiple section tabs. The active section tab highlighted. Users can go back and forth checking the information entered.

(c) Grouping billing and personal information in the desktop version to avoid unecessary scrolling while customer inputs sensitive data. It is displayed such that the customer can view all details at a glance.

COLOR SCHEME

I have retained the colour scheme to reflect the colours chosen by the company. The color combination of BLACK and WHITE keeps the design simple while radiating elegance. The GREEN brings a pop-factor while highlighting the fact that the company promotes responsible consumption of renewable or green energy resources.

FONT FAMILY

The font I chose for this design is a Google font - PT Sans. It is a san-serif font that makes it easy to read while giving a reassuring approachable feeling.


WHAT I LEARNED FROM THIS CASE STUDY

  • For short sprints like these it is important to recognise scope .

  • When designing part of a system, it is important to pay attention to the language and mood of the system.That has to come through in your part of the design.

  • You cannot ignore the importance of research. Even when designing part of the system, understanding the product well can help you design effectively for the user.
